But what of Fabaceae?

Questions:
What is the distribution of incompatibility in Fabaceae?
Does Fabaceae also share this potentially homologous SI system?


This SI system is older than the Fabales and Rosales lineages.

The majority of investigated species in Fabaceae exhibit phenotypic presentation of many characteristics common to this SI system.

It has been suggested that angiosperm families are generally characterized by one type of SI mechanism.

and yet...

We have...


gathered and summarized data from 400+ published reports.

created a database with 1285 unique species — 6.6% of those recognized in the family — across 190 genera spanning five of the six currently recognized subfamilies.

uncovered the same bimodal distribution of selfing and outcrossing as in several other angiosperm families.

found no evidence indicating that (1) Fabaceae shares the same SI system as Rosaceae, or even (2) that one system is characteristic to the family.

But what of Orchidaceae?

We have...


gathered and summarized data from 600+ published reports.

created a database with 1107 unique species — 3.95% of those recognized in the family — across 255 genera spanning all five subfamilies.

uncovered the same bimodal distribution of selfing and outcrossing as in several other angiosperm families.

highlighted an important need for genetic and molecular analyses of monocot SI mechanism(s).
